Direct Way Cargo Offering Aid to Earthquake Victims

2015 Nepal Earthquake update

We are all aware of the devastating 7.8 earthquake that ravaged Kathmandu and large portions of Nepal on April 25th. 8.1 million people are said to be affected by the Earthquake.

While many people in Kathmandu city have returned home to their families villages the CEO of Direct Way Exporters, Amir Bagale, elected to stay. He sent his brother home to their family with money, plastic sheeting for the missing rooftops and supplies. Amir stayed in Kathmandu city during the aftermath to help any person that needed it.

Direct Way Exporters offered free phone calls, WiFi, water, food and support from their offices in Thamel Kathmandu city. See a map to Direct Way here.

Amir also offered help to anyone looking for missing or displaced persons within Kathmandu city.

Throughout 2015/2016 Direct Way Exporters has been directly involved in helping to bring supplies to remote communities in Nepal. These supplies ranged from food and shelter to blankets, school supplies and medical equipment.

Direct Way Exporters are willing to help anyone who wishes to send goods or aid to those affected by the earthquake.
